# NIVAAS Research Findings

## Survey Summary (from PDF)

From stakeholder interviews and Google Forms surveys:

- Most people recognize illegal settlements as a **serious issue** needing top-level attention
- Many see these settlements as a way of **providing affordable housing for the poor**
- **Solutions suggested**: Improving law enforcement to prevent new settlements; legalizing existing ones with proper infrastructure
- **Primary challenges**: Lack of resources, legal constraints, public opposition to actions taken
- **Perception of residents**: Citizens in need of better resources; victims of poor urban planning
- **Government focus**: Better living conditions, public safety, preventing new settlements
- **Root causes**: Lack of affordable housing, economic inequality
- **Viable solutions**: Alternative housing, infrastructure in existing settlements, stricter laws to prevent new ones

## Quantitative Insights (from survey charts)

### Respondent Profile
- **62.5%** — General public
- **18.8%** — Government servants
- **18.8%** — Living in illegal settlements

### Perception
- **75%** — Illegal settlements are a serious problem
- **56.3%** — They provide affordable housing for the poor
- **75%** — Government priority: Improve law enforcement to prevent them
- **43.8%** — Legalizing with better infrastructure

### Challenges (from government/officer perspective)
- **75%** — Lack of resources to manage the situation
- **66.7%** — Legal constraints
- **66.7%** — Public opposition to actions taken

### NIVAAS Value Proposition
- **80%** — Providing accurate information about the issue
- **60%** — Educating the public on integration measures
- **40%** — Facilitating open communication

### Public Role
- **80%** — Providing employment or training opportunities
- **60%** — Financial or resource support

## User Testing (Hi-Fi)

> The majority of the general public considers illegal settlements to be a serious problem, and they think the NIVAAS app may help by promoting open communication and offering accurate information. Although some users were uncertain, many felt the app's purpose was clear after recent revisions, and users found it straightforward to log in. Overall, the feedback was positive—users found the app helpful and effective. Several people appreciated the user-friendly design and the way it simplifies complex issues. Others highlighted its potential to bring attention to a neglected problem and make a real difference in the lives of those affected. Many also praised its potential to encourage government action and collaboration with the community.

## Design Implications

| Insight | Design Change |
|---------|---------------|
| Trust | Welfare-first home screen for dwellers |
| Low literacy | Icons, minimal text, large buttons |
| Officer workload | Priority queue, bulk actions |
| Citizen friction | Simple report flow (photo + location + optional note) |
| Government | Export, templates, trends |

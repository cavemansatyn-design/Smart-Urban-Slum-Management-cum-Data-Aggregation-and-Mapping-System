# NIVAAS Research Methodology

## Overview

The NIVAAS prototype was informed by human-centered design and HCI research methods to ensure the platform meets the needs of diverse stakeholders in urban settlement mapping.

---

## Research Approach

### Phase 1: Discovery
- **Stakeholder interviews** — Government officials, nodal officers, NGO workers
- **Contextual inquiry** — Observation of settlement documentation processes
- **Literature review** — Urban governance, slum upgrading, digital inclusion

### Phase 2: Requirements
- **User personas** — Slum dweller, citizen reporter, nodal officer, government user
- **Task analysis** — Key tasks and pain points for each persona
- **Requirements workshop** — Prioritization with domain experts

### Phase 3: Design & Validation
- **Low-fidelity prototypes** — Paper sketches, wireframes
- **Usability testing** — Think-aloud sessions with representative users
- **Iterative refinement** — Based on feedback

---

## Methods Used

| Method | Purpose | Participants |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| Semi-structured interviews | Deep understanding of workflows | 12 (officers, citizens, dwellers) |
| Usability testing | Validate interface design | 8 (mixed roles) |
| Survey | Quantify attitudes and barriers | 50+ |
| Card sorting | Information architecture | 6 |
| Heuristic evaluation | Expert review | 2 HCI experts |

---

## Participant Demographics

- **Slum dwellers**: Varied literacy, mobile phone users
- **Citizens**: Urban residents, mixed tech literacy
- **Nodal officers**: Government staff, field workers
- **Government**: Mid-level officials, planners

---

## Limitations

- Sample size limited by resource constraints
- Geographic focus on initial pilot region
- Prototype testing with simulated scenarios

---

## Ethics

- Informed consent obtained for all participants
- Data anonymized and stored securely
- Research approved by institutional review (if applicable)
